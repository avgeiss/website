About Me

I am a PhD candidate in the Department of Atmospheric Sciences at the University of Washington in Seattle. My primary research involves investigating the relationship between clouds and climate on monthly to decadal time-scales, using a combination of satellite cloud observations and climate models. Other areas of interest include tropical atmospheric dynamics, atmospheric boundary layer dynamics, satellite remote sensing, image processing, machine-learning and AI.

Recent:

03-06-2018:
New paper accepted for publication in Atmospheric Chemistry and Physics. In this paper we investigate trends in satellite observations of cloud occurrence over the world's extratropical oceans between 2000-2013. We find connections between trends in specific cloud types and trends in extratropical meteorology, that are ultimately linked to modes of extratropical climate variability (e.g. the southern annular mode):

Geiss, A. and Marchand, R. (2019) Cloud responses to climate variability over the extratropical oceans as observed by MISR and MODIS, Atmospheric Chemistry and Physics, https://doi.org/10.5194/acp-19-7547-2019

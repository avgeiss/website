About Me

I am a Postdoctoral Research Associate in the Atmospheric Sciences and Global Change Division at Pacific Northwest National Laboratory in Richland WA. My primary research involves AI and machine learning applications in atmospheric science. Other areas of interest include climate science and cloud-climate interaction, tropical atmospheric dynamics, atmospheric boundary layer dynamics, satellite remote sensing, and image processing/computer vision.

Recent

6 May 2021:
A preprint is now available for our recent work on radar inpainting. We use deep learning and generative adversarial networks to fill-in regions of damaged or missing radar data.

Geiss, A. and Hardin, J. C. (2021) Inpainting Radar Missing Data Regions with Deep Learning. Atmospheric Measurement Techniques Discussion, (preprint - in review), https://doi.org/10.5194/amt-2021-100

1 Dec 2020:
In this recent paper we use deep convolutional neural networks to enhance the resolution of radar data. This work was featured as a paper of note in the Februaury 2021 issue of BAMS.

Geiss, A. and Hardin, J. C. (2020) Radar Super Resolution using a Deep Convolutional Neural Network. Journal of Atmospheric and Oceanic Technology, 37-12: 2197-2207, https://doi.org/10.1175/JTECH-D-20-0074.1

11 Nov 2020:
A preprint is available on arXiv of our recent work on physically consistent deep-learning-based super resolution. Here, we develop a method to ensure that physical conservation laws are strictly enforced when using a CNN for super resolution.

Geiss, A. and Hardin, J. C. (2020) Invertible CNN-Based Super Resolution with Downsampling Awareness. arXiv, (preprint - in review), https://arxiv.org/abs/2011.05586

About Me

I am a Postdoctoral Research Associate in the Atmospheric Sciences and Global Change Division at Pacific Northwest National Laboratory in Richland WA. My primary research involves AI and machine learning applications for radars and in atmospheric science. Other areas of interest include climate science and cloud-climate interaction, tropical atmospheric dynamics, atmospheric boundary layer dynamics, satellite remote sensing, and image processing/computer vision.

Recent:

02-18-2020:
New paper accepted for publication in Geophysical Research Letters: In this work, we use global satellite records of ocean temperature and cloudiness to investigate connections between sea surface temperature reemergence, a tendency for wintertime ocean temperatures to be influenced by ocean temperature from the previous winter, and marine stratus cloud amount. We identify a region in the North Pacific Ocean where wintertime cloud amount is related to sea surface temperature from the prior winter:

Geiss, A., Marchand, R., and Thompson, L. (2020) The Influence of Sea Surface Temperature Reemergence on Marine Stratiform Cloud, Geophysical Research Letters, https://doi.org/10.1029/2020GL086957
